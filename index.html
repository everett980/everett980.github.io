Cranky Coaster

Cranky Coaster is an intelligent coaster that uses a force sensitive resistor connected to an Edison to monitor how much water you drink. This data is then stored and compared to how much water you should be drinking. If you are continually drinking enough water, you'll automatically be praised on Twitter for your valiant efforts. Fail to drink enough water and you will be publicly shamed for everyone to know about your inability to take care of yourself! Additionally, you will receive a text every hour to make sure you are acutely aware of your water consumption. Users can also access a website for visualizing your current day of water consumption in great deal, as well as a brief overview of the last five days of water consumption. Lastly, you can check a native iOS app so that you can quickly check in on your status and educate yourself if you ought to go for another sip of water. Cranky Coaster will ensure you take good care of yourself!

Built in 36 hours at Bitcamp with Yustynn Panicker
Winner of Booz Allen Hamilton's Best Internet of Things (IoT) Hack
Winner of BookHolder's Longest Stank Name

Girder Gus

Girder Gus is a 2D Web Platformer. Unlike most platform protagonists, Gus cannot jump. Instead, in order to cross perrilous gaps between platforms, Gus can place girders to build bridges. Additionally, when Gus walks into a wall, the world rotates so that the wall becomes the floor! By utilizing these two mechanics, players can overcome mind-bending puzzles. If you fail an attempt at a level, on your next playthrough a ghost will reenact your last attempt.

Our project's goal was not just to make an incredibly fun game, we also wanted to provide a platform on which anyone could design their own levels. Our level editor allows anyone to easily build, test, and publish their own challenging creations. Crafting levels would soon lose its appeal if you were the only one who could play your levels, so we built a social network that allows users to leave feedback on a level, as well as follow a creator if they want to see more from that user. Successful creators who earn lots of stars or followers are featured on our top creators page!

Built as a capstone project at Fullstack Academy of Code with David Reeve, Brian Dunn, and Yustynn Panicker

RoomEase

RoomEase lets users define what chores have to be done and how frequently. These chores get randomly distributed amongst all the roommates. If you get a chore you particularly dislike, you can propose to trade with your roommate. You can swap it for another chore, offer to pay someone money to do it for you, or both! At the end of the month, our scheduled job runner checks how much each user has to pay for rent after factoring in many exchanges, and automatically transfers money from each of your roommates to whoever pays the rent. This saves you from having to remind your buddies that they owe you money if they do not have the right amount on them. It's completely automatic, and happens as a part of your normal rent payment so it's very convenient.

Built in 36 hours at HackPrinceton with Yustynn Panicker and Leon Daniel Thorne
This was the first project any of us made using the React ecosystem
